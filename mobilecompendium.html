About

The Mobile Compendium is a mobile application for the PC game Dragon's Prophet, which helps players keep track of the dragons they capture in-game, and share their progress with their friends. In addition its tracking function, the application also improves upon the in-game 'catalogue' of dragons, showing players where certain dragons can be obtained, and what they look like.

Technology

  • Front-End: HTML5, CSS3, Ionic
  • Developing in a HTML environment makes for easier troubleshooting of technical issues without having to go back and forth between the coding and mobile environment. The Ionic framework also allows for the application to have a native mobile appearance, and allows for the use of HTML, CSS, and AngularJS to develop applications.
  • Framework: AngularJS
  • AngularJS introduces a relatively clean MVC framework that allows for predefined variables to be used to speed up the creation of the application, as well as resulting in neater code that's easier to navigate and show to others, should other contributors become involved. It also allows for Single-Page Functionality to mimic the navigation of a native app.
  • Back-End: AngularJS, Server-Side PHP & MySQL
  • AngularJS is also used in the back-end, replacing the need for client-side PHP (which does not function on an MVC framework as it's minimal) by communicating to a server-side database through JSON-encoded strings. PHP and MySQL make up the server-side database, receiving and sending requests through JSON-encoded strings.

Features

  • Track your in-game progress of collected and missing dragons in game, and share your progress with your friends through the app
  • Browse through a complete library of dragons found in the game, and filter your search based on family, region, and colour to find your next capture
  • Research detailed information about every dragon in the game, including their appearance, family, compendium entry, and where to find them
